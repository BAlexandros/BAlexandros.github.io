This page is a summary of my education. This includes formal education, like courses taken in university, and informal like extracurricular courses taken for personal growth

Formal Education

My formal education is in the field of Physics, with courses and projects related to programming.

BSc in Physics

I started my undergraduate studies in 2014, at the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, and received my BSc in Physics in 2019 with a GPA of 8.06/10.

Notable courses and academic projects that defined my undergraduate studies are the following:

  • Computer Programming and Computational Physics

    An introductory course to programming in the context of physics. This course, taught in C, explored basic IO operations, built-in data structures such as arrays and structs, and manual memory management. Most importantly, it served as my first contact with programming and sparked my drive to continue learning on my own.

  • Computational Physics and Applications

    A higher level course which required the development of programs to simulate Solid State Physics and Atmospheric Physics phenomena. The language required for the accepted solutions was MATLAB, which despite having limited presence in non-academic environments proved a very effective tool for scientific projects.

  • Theoretical Statistical Solid State Physics

    Other than its intimidating name, this course was characterized by extensive simulations of Random Number Generators and Random Walks. Using Mathematica, RNG formulas were implemented and studied in the context of Random Walks and Solid State Physics phenomena (such as phase changes).

  • Bioelectromagnetics

    A very interesting course that explored the effects of non-ionizing radiation (radio, microwave, etc) on the human body and potential medical applications. This course greatly enhances the understanding of terminology, phenomena, and the principles which dictate how modern devices affect (or don't) the human body. Despite the fact that it required no coding, the phenomena studied heavily depend on simulations and one of them became the basis of my thesis.

The final and most extensive project during my years in the department was my undergraduate thesis, titled "Magnetic nanoparticle hyperthermia treatment planning optimized for tissue sparing" and supervised by professor Theodoros Samaras. The research interests of professor Samaras allowed for the undertaking of a thesis which combined my interests in programming and bioelectromagnetics. Developing an extensive program in MATLAB, it was possible to simulate and study a large number of cancer treatments to determine the optimal approach. This project later evolved into a paper, which is described in the website's publications page.

MSc in Computational Physics

I started my MSc in Computational Physics in 2020. My background in physics and my interest in programming led me to choose an MSc program that combines both. These courses are currently attended online, with a schedule that facilitates full time employment. My current GPA is 9.83/10.0.

Some notable courses are the following:

  • Data Analysis And Processing with MATLAB

    This course focused on the statistical analysis of data. MATLAB was used to study distributions, perform testing of hypotheses, and study trends.

  • Programming Languages

    Application of various sorting algorithms in C/C++, and familiarization with the Visual Studio Code IDE.

  • Computational Electromagnetics

    Analysis and implementation of the algorithms used to study phenomena related to electromagnetic waves. As the main course project, a MATLAB program was created to implement Yee's algorithm and simulate a microwave cavity. Additionally, a statistical analysis was performed to study the microwave oven's ability to heat its contents uniformly.

Other courses

These are courses taken independently from the formal education described above. What is listed may not offer certificates upon completion, but follows a hands-on approach that is very beneficial to personal growth. They are rotated in such a way that progress is made weekly on all of them.

The ability to handle and understand data structures can greatly improve the way problems are approached and solved. This course provides a hands-on approach and requires the users to reimplement data structures from scratch, while simultaneously getting familiar with Java and Test Driven Development.

Full Stack Open

A complete curriculum for becoming a full stack Javascript developer. This course teaches the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express, React.js, Node.js).

Android Basics using Kotlin

Mobile applications are an indispensible part of our daily lives. From the hobbyist looking to make an app to facilitate their daily routine, to a developer of a global corporation looking to reach out to its clients, the ability to materialize these ideas can be very benificial. I therefore decided to begin this course in order to learn the basics of developing apps and have the ability to bring some of my ideas to life. Kotlin was chosen over Java because it is the modern standard for Android development, and my Java skills are being worked on with the CS61B course.
